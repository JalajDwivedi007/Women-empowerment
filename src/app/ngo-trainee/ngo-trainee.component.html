NGOs


1) Shri Kalyan Women Welfare Society

Founded in 1996.

The Kalyan Women Welfare Society is a non-profit organisation dedicated to upgrading people in Rajasthan's most dangerous geographical districts, where there is severe water scarcity and poverty. The foundation offers free education, skill development training, and job opportunities.

In collaboration with government and non-government organisations, the women's NGO aims for child rights, legal protection, improved health and welfare, access to clean water, and nutritional food.



2) Rajasthan Samgrah Kalyan Sansthan

Founded in 1992.

RSKS (Rajasthan Samgrah Kalyan Sansthan) is a grassroots nonprofit organisation dedicated to assisting marginalised women and girls. The women's NGO supports empowerment, sustainable livelihood, girls' education, gender equality, child rights, and the welfare of the community's marginalised and impoverished members.

The charity also works to tackle issues concerning agriculture, the ecology, and water shortages. The United Nations Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC) has accorded this NGO for women and girls Special Consultative Status (2015).



3) Gramin Vikas Evam Chetna Sansthan

Founded in 1998.

The goal of this women's NGO is to empower and elevate rural and disadvantaged areas, assist women, encourage craftsmen via their trade, and produce income. Gramin Vikas Evam Chetan Sansthan (GVCS) was established in the town of Barmer, Rajasthan. It is dedicated to empowering women artisans by making handicrafts a viable home-based source of income for financial independence.

GVCS began with just ten members and has since evolved to a large-scale organisation that serves over 22,000 women in 75 communities. A expanding network of women artisans working in various craft forms has enabled GVCS to work on a wide range of rural development initiatives such as the construction of self-help groups or SHGs, the provision of fodder for drought-affected cattle, and educational programmes for children and women.



4) Swadhar IDWC (Institute for Development of Women and Children)

Founded in 1983.

Swadhar was formed in Mumbai in 1983 to assist women in need, regardless of caste, religion, community, or financial background. Swadhar IDWC was formed in 1998 as Swadhar added daycare to its services. It began focusing on education, health care, and general development of poor children, with a specific emphasis on the female child, in addition to tackling women-centric concerns.

Swadhar, according to its name, works tirelessly to create self-reliant women and children by educating them about their rights and allowing them to live independent, dignified lives.
