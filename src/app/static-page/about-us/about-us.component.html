About the Ministry


Introduction

The Department of Women and Child Development, Government of India, was established as a distinct Ministry on January 30, 2006, having previously been a Department within the Ministry of Human Resources Development since 1985.
The Ministry was established with the primary goal of resolving gaps in State action for women and children and supporting inter-Ministerial and inter-sectoral convergence in order to develop gender equitable and child-centered laws, policies, and programmes.

Vision and Mission

Vision

Empowered women living with dignity and participating as equal contributors in development of an environment which is free of violence and prejudice. And well-nurtured youngsters with many opportunities for growth and development in a safe and protected environment.

Mission

Promoting women's social and economic empowerment by cross-cutting policies and programmes, mainstreaming gender problems, raising knowledge of their rights, and providing institutional and legislative support to enable them to achieve their human rights and reach their full potential. Also, ensuring children's growth, care, and protection by cross-cutting policies and programmes, raising knowledge about their rights, and facilitating access to learning, nutrition, institutional, and legislative assistance to allow them to grow and develop to their full potential.

Organisation

The Ministry has been implementing the world's largest and most unique and outreach programme of Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS) for the holistic development of the child, providing a package of services including supplementary nutrition, immunisation, health check up and referral services, and pre-school non-formal education. The Ministry's programmes are mostly managed by non-governmental organisations. Attempts are being undertaken to increase the effectiveness of NGOs' participation.
